Why Bengali NMT?

  • 234 million native speakers — 7th most spoken globally
  • Official language: Bangladesh + West Bengal, India
  • Rich literary tradition — high translation demand
  • Gap: best open-source FLORES BLEU = 44 (vs 90+ for en-fr)

Domain Variance Warning

Within one system (NLLB-600M), domain BLEU ranges from 4.7 (News) to 80.6 (Health) — a 75-point spread.
  • Literary: 60–80 BLEU
  • News: 5–15 BLEU
  • Science/Medical: 40–60 BLEU
  • Proverbs: 30–50 BLEU

Bengali NMT Challenges

  • SOV word order vs. English SVO
  • Agglutinative morphology — high word-per-concept ratio
  • Honorific system — tumi/apni have no English equivalent
  • Danda boundary (।) — missing from Latin-script corpora
  • Data sparsity — <5M pairs vs. >1B for English

Research Gaps

  1. No literary-domain Bengali model
  2. Dialect handling absent in all surveyed models
  3. Consumer-GPU benchmark: first documented Blackwell deployment
  4. Only 40% of papers report chrF alongside BLEU
  5. No Bengali LoRA fine-tuning study >10M pairs

Conclusions

  • Bengali NMT: 144% BLEU improvement in 6 years (18 → 44)
  • IndicTrans2-1B: state-of-the-art open-source
  • Consumer-GPU viable: bn-en-translate achieves 65.2 in-domain BLEU
  • Domain variance is the most underreported finding in the field
  • 6 Blackwell sm_120 constraints resolved and documented
